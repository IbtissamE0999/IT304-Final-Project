Critical Ethical Analysis of Deepfake Technology

📋 Instructions for Team Member 4

Your goal: Apply TWO different ethical frameworks to analyze deepfake technology and the application examples from the previous sections. Show how different ethical perspectives lead to different conclusions.

Required ethical lenses (choose 2):

  • Consequentialism/Utilitarianism: Focus on outcomes weighing harms vs. benefits, greatest good for greatest number
  • Deontological/Rights-Based Ethics: Focus on duties, rules, and rights consent, dignity, respect for persons regardless of outcomes
  • Virtue Ethics: Focus on character what kind of society/people do we want to be? What virtues should guide technology development?
  • Care Ethics: Focus on relationships, vulnerability, and responsibility who is harmed? How do we protect the vulnerable?
  • Justice/Fairness: Focus on distribution of benefits/harms who benefits from deepfakes? Who bears the costs? Are impacts equitable?

Recommended combination: Consequentialism + Rights-Based Ethics (these create interesting contrasts)

What to include:

  • Introduction: Briefly explain why we need ethical frameworks to evaluate deepfakes
  • Lens 1 explanation: What is this ethical framework? What does it prioritize? What questions does it ask?
  • Lens 1 analysis: How does this lens evaluate deepfakes and the applications discussed? What conclusions does it reach?
  • Lens 2 explanation: What is this ethical framework? What does it prioritize? What questions does it ask?
  • Lens 2 analysis: How does this lens evaluate deepfakes and the applications discussed? What conclusions does it reach?
  • Comparison: How do the two lenses differ? Where do they agree/disagree? Why does the choice of lens matter?

Suggested structure:

  • Intro paragraph on the need for ethical analysis (1 paragraph)
  • Ethical Lens 1: Explanation + Application (2-3 paragraphs)
  • Ethical Lens 2: Explanation + Application (2-3 paragraphs)
  • Comparative Analysis (1-2 paragraphs)
  • Brief conclusion (1 paragraph)

Images/Graphics: 1-2 images (could be diagrams comparing the ethical frameworks, infographics, or relevant illustrations)

Key point: Make sure to apply each lens to the actual deepfake examples from Application 1 and 2. Don't just define the frameworks show how they lead to different evaluations of the same technology.
