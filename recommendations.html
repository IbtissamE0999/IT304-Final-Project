Social Implications and Recommendations for Mitigating Harm

📋 Instructions for Team Member 5

Your goal: Discuss the broader social implications of deepfakes, identify key stakeholders, and provide concrete recommendations for using the technology more responsibly.

What to include:

  • Social implications: What are the broader political and social concerns? What innate harms or risks does deepfake technology pose to society?
  • Stakeholder identification: Who are the main audiences for this analysis? Who needs to care and why?
  • Recommendations organized by stakeholder type:
    • Policy makers / Government
    • Technology companies / Platform operators
    • Content creators / Media organizations
    • Educators / Digital literacy advocates
    • Individual citizens / General public
  • Types of solutions to cover:
    • Technological solutions (detection tools, watermarking, authentication)
    • Policy/legal recommendations (legislation, regulation)
    • Social/educational strategies (media literacy, public awareness)
  • Future outlook: What does the future look like if we don't address these issues? What if we do?

Suggested structure:

  • Introduction: Overview of social implications (1 paragraph)
  • Political and Social Concerns (1-2 paragraphs)
  • Stakeholder Analysis (1 paragraph)
  • Recommendations by Category (3-4 paragraphs, one per stakeholder group or solution type)
  • Future Outlook (1 paragraph)
  • Conclusion (1 paragraph)

Images/Graphics: 2-3 images (could be infographics about solutions, diagrams of stakeholder relationships, charts showing detection technology, etc.)

Important: Make recommendations specific and actionable. Don't just say "we need better laws" say what those laws should do. Don't just say "improve detection" explain what detection approaches show promise.
