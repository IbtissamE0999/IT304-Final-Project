1. Deepfakes in Media & Entertainment

The entertainment industry has been an early adopter of "synthetic media." Deepfake technology allows for high-fidelity de-aging and digital resurrection. Filmmakers can now seamlessly blend the likeness of an older actor onto a younger body double or recreate the performances of actors who have passed away. While this offers immense creative freedom and saves costs on heavy prosthetics, it raises questions about the future of digital likeness rights.

2. Deepfake Fraud and Cybercrime

A more sinister application of this technology is found in financial fraud. "Business Email Compromise" (BEC) has evolved into "Deepfake Voice/Video Compromise." Criminals use AI-generated audio or video to impersonate high-level executives (CEOs) to authorize fraudulent wire transfers. These attacks are highly effective because they exploit the victim's trust and the authority of the person being impersonated.

Figure 2: The increasing need for rigorous fact-checking in the age of synthetic misinformation.

3. Social Media & Misinformation

On social media, deepfakes are weaponized to spread misinformation. A prominent case study is the manipulated video of President Volodymyr Zelenskyy, which appeared to show him telling Ukrainian soldiers to surrender. This demonstrates how deepfakes can be used as a tool of information warfare, designed to destabilize organizations and cause public panic before the truth can be verified.
