Real-World Applications: Media Uses & Fraud in Deepfake Technology

Written by: Ibtissam

Introduction: Dual Nature of Deepfakes

Deepfake technology has both positive and negative applications in modern society. While it is widely used in entertainment and media production, it is also increasingly exploited for fraud, scams, and identity theft. This dual-use nature makes it one of the most controversial AI technologies today.

Entertainment & Film Industry Uses

In the entertainment industry, deepfakes are used to improve visual effects, de-age actors, and even recreate historical or deceased figures. This reduces production costs and allows filmmakers to create more realistic scenes.

Figure 1: Face-swapping technology in deepfakes

Source: Wikimedia Commons

For example, movie studios have used AI-based facial mapping to digitally de-age actors or recreate younger versions of characters, making storytelling more flexible and visually advanced.

Fraud & Identity Theft Cases

One of the most dangerous uses of deepfake technology is financial fraud. Criminals use AI-generated voices and videos to impersonate executives, employees, or even family members.

Figure 2: AI systems used to generate synthetic media

Source: Wikimedia Commons

In several reported cases, attackers cloned a CEO’s voice to instruct employees to transfer money, resulting in major financial losses. These scams are becoming more realistic and harder to detect.

Broader Social Impact

Deepfake fraud not only causes financial damage but also reduces trust in digital communication. As technology improves, people may begin to doubt even legitimate audio and video evidence.

Figure 3: Digital scam and phishing concept

Source: Wikimedia Commons

This creates a serious challenge for cybersecurity, law enforcement, and online platforms trying to verify authenticity.
