1. Deepfakes in Media & Entertainment

The entertainment industry uses deepfake technology for high-fidelity de-aging and digital resurrection. Filmmakers can recreate the performances of actors or restore their youthful appearance, as seen in major franchises like Star Wars. While this offers creative freedom, it introduces ethical concerns regarding the digital rights of deceased performers.

2. Deepfake Fraud and Cybercrime

Criminals are increasingly using AI-generated voices and videos to impersonate high-level executives or family members. This leads to sophisticated financial scams where employees are tricked into authorizing large wire transfers, a process known as Business Email Compromise (BEC).

Figure 2: Representation of digital identity theft and synthetic media fraud.

3. Social Media Misinformation

Deepfakes are a major tool for spreading misinformation on social media. By creating realistic but entirely fake footage of public figures, bad actors can manipulate public opinion and damage the credibility of news sources. Detection software and media literacy are becoming essential to combat these digital manipulations.
